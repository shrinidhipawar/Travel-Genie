KOLKATA TRAVEL GUIDE

OVERVIEW:
Kolkata is the capital of West Bengal, known as the "City of Joy". It's famous for its colonial architecture, rich cultural heritage, literature, and authentic Bengali cuisine.

TOP ATTRACTIONS:

1. Victoria Memorial - Stunning white marble monument and museum, iconic landmark of Kolkata
2. Indian Museum - Oldest and largest museum in India with artifacts, mummies, and historical exhibits
3. Dakshineswar Kali Temple - Sacred Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Kali on the banks of Hooghly River
4. Belur Math - Headquarters of Ramakrishna Mission with beautiful architecture and peaceful grounds
5. Birla Mandir - White marble temple dedicated to Lord Krishna with impressive design
6. College Street - Historic street lined with bookstores, cafes, and intellectual atmosphere
7. Park Street - Vibrant street with shops, restaurants, bars, and nightlife
8. Hooghly River - Beautiful river with evening strolls and sunset views
9. Indian Botanic Garden - 275-acre garden with rare plants and the largest banyan tree in the world
10. St. Paul's Cathedral - Historic Gothic cathedral with stunning architecture

RESTAURANTS & FOOD:
- Luchi Aloo - Puffed bread with potato curry (breakfast staple)
- Kosha Mangsho - Slow-cooked mutton with spices (Kolkata specialty)
- Maacher Jhol - Fish curry with mustard and turmeric
- Doi Maach - Fish cooked in yogurt-based gravy
- Chingri Malai Curry - Prawns in coconut milk
- Hilsa Fish - Regional specialty, delicate white fish
- Rosogolla - Sweet cottage cheese dumplings in sugar syrup
- Sandesh - Traditional Bengali sweet
- Phuchka/Pani Puri - Street food with spiced water and tamarind
- Jhal Muri - Spicy puffed rice snack
- Restaurants: 6 Ballygunge Place, Aaheli, Oh! Calcutta, Peter Cat, Mocambo

SHOPPING:
- Gariahat Market - Traditional market for clothes, spices, and daily items
- New Market - Multi-story shopping complex with clothes and accessories
- College Street - Books, stationery, and vintage items
- Tangra - Chinese food and handicraft items
- Sarojini Saree Mart - Traditional Bengali sarees
- Dakshin - Handicrafts and textiles from South India

LOCAL EXPERIENCES:
- Durga Puja Festival (September-October) - Biggest festival with decorated pandals
- Ramakrishna Mission - Spiritual and cultural hub
- Literary Tours - Visit birthplaces of famous Bengali writers
- Traditional Kathak Dance - Classical dance performances
- Bengali Handicrafts - Visit artisans' workshops

TRANSPORT:
- Metro Rail - Efficient public transport system
- Trams - Historic and scenic tram rides (unique to Kolkata)
- Taxis/Cabs - Ola and Uber available
- Autos - Auto-rickshaws for short distances
- Buses - Government and private buses
- Ferries - Across Hooghly River

MUSEUMS:
- Indian Museum - Artifacts and history
- Rabindra Sarovar - Cultural center
- Birla Museum - Art and sculpture
- Academy of Fine Arts - Contemporary art

NEARBY DAY TRIPS:
- Darjeeling - 600km, tea gardens and mountain views (2-3 days)
- Sundarbans - 100km, tiger reserve and mangrove forests
- Shantiniketan - 150km, university and cultural center
- Nabadwip - 100km, pilgrimage site

ACCOMMODATION:
- Budget hotels - Rs 800-1500/night
- Mid-range - Rs 2000-4000/night
- Heritage hotels - Rs 3000+/night

BUDGET TIPS:
- Street food very cheap (Rs 30-100)
- Metro and trams affordable (Rs 6-15)
- Many temples free entry
- Museums with minimal fees (Rs 50-100)
- Haggle in local markets for better prices

CULTURAL FACTS:
- Kolkata produced famous figures: Tagore, Roy, Bose, etc.
- Known for printing and publishing industry
- Coffee culture - traditional adda (gathering places)
- Film industry (Bengali cinema)
