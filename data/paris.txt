PARIS TRAVEL GUIDE

OVERVIEW:
Paris is the capital of France, known as the "City of Light". It's famous for art, romance, cuisine, and iconic landmarks.

TOP ATTRACTIONS:

1. Louvre Museum - One of the world's largest museums, home to the Mona Lisa and many masterpieces. Located in the 1st arrondissement. Best to visit in the morning to avoid lines. Nearby cafes include Cafe Marly.

2. Montmartre & Sacré-Cœur - Historic district with artsy streets, views of Paris from the Sacré-Cœur basilica. Good for afternoon walks. Try local bistros on Rue Lepic.

3. Le Marais - Trendy neighborhood with boutiques, museums (e.g., Musée Picasso), and many kosher and falafel restaurants like L'As du Fallafel.

4. Musée d'Orsay - Impressionist art museum located on the Seine. Open late on Thursdays. Great for art lovers.

5. Saint-Germain-des-Prés - Elegant area with cafes like Café de Flore. Good for evening strolls and dinner.

6. Eiffel Tower - Iconic iron lattice monument, best visited at sunset

7. Notre-Dame Cathedral - Historic Gothic cathedral on Île de la Cité

HIDDEN GEMS:
- Canal Saint-Martin - Scenic canals with cafes
- Rue Crémieux - Colorful Instagram-famous street
- Musée de l'Orangerie - Monet's Water Lilies

RESTAURANTS & FOOD:
- Le Comptoir du Relais - Classic French cuisine, famous for escargots
- Angelina - Famous for pastries and hot chocolate
- L'As du Fallafel - Popular Middle Eastern spot in Le Marais
- Café de Flore - Historic café in Saint-Germain-des-Prés
- Ladurée - Famous for macarons

BEST TIME TO VISIT:
April-May and September-October (pleasant weather)

TRANSPORT:
Paris Metro is efficient and affordable. Buses also available.

TIPS:
- Book Louvre tickets online to skip queues
- Many museums free on first Sunday of month
- Learn basic French phrases
