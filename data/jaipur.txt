JAIPUR TRAVEL GUIDE

OVERVIEW:
Jaipur is the capital of Rajasthan, known as the "Pink City" for its unique pink buildings. It's famous for palaces, forts, vibrant markets, and royal Rajasthani culture.

TOP ATTRACTIONS:

1. City Palace - Still partially royal residence, stunning blend of Mughal and Rajasthani architecture
2. Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds) - Iconic 5-story pink structure with 953 small windows
3. Jantar Mantar - UNESCO heritage astronomical observation site
4. Albert Hall Museum - Museum with art, artifacts, and historical items
5. Govind Dev Ji Temple - Sacred Hindu temple with evening aarti
6. Rambagh Palace - Former royal palace, now a luxury hotel
7. Jal Mahal - Beautiful palace surrounded by water (photo spot)

FORTS & PALACES:
1. Nahargarh Fort - Historic fort with panoramic city views at sunset
2. Albert Hall - Victorian architecture museum
3. Chaugan Stadium - Historic polo ground

RESTAURANTS & FOOD:
- Gatte ki Sabzi - Yogurt and gram flour vegetable
- Ker Sangri - Desert vegetables cooked with spices
- Laal Maas - Spicy meat curry (Rajasthani specialty)
- Bajra Roti - Pearl millet flatbread
- Dal Baati Churma - Baked wheat balls with lentils and sweet crumble
- Mirchi Vada - Spicy green chili fritters
- Chaach - Traditional buttermilk drink
- Restaurants: Laxmi Mishtan Bhandar (traditional), Handi (fine dining)

SHOPPING:
- Jaipur City Palace Market - Textiles, jewelry, and handicrafts
- Bapu Bazaar - Budget shopping for fabrics and clothes
- Johari Bazaar - Jewelry market, be careful of prices
- Kishanpol Bazaar - Antiques and handicrafts
- M.I. Road - Modern shops and restaurants
- Rajwada Shopping Mall - Contemporary shopping
- Handloom textiles - Rajasthani prints and designs

LOCAL EXPERIENCES:
- Puppet Show (Kathputli) - Traditional Rajasthani puppet theater
- Cooking Classes - Learn Rajasthani cuisine
- Pottery Workshops - Visit artisans
- Camel Safari - Desert experience (nearby)
- Turban Tying - Cultural experience

TRANSPORT:
- Autos - Cheap local transport
- Taxis - Ola and Uber available
- Cabs - Tourist taxis for sightseeing
- Cycles - For exploring small areas
- Buses - Government and private buses

MUSEUMS:
- Albert Hall Museum - Historical artifacts
- Government Central Museum - Art and history
- Jantar Mantar - Astronomical instruments

NEARBY DAY TRIPS:
- Amber Fort - 11km, iconic Rajasthani fort
- Jal Mahal - 6km, palace in the lake
- Nahargarh Fort - 6km, sunset views
- Sariska Tiger Reserve - 100km, wildlife sanctuary

ACCOMMODATION:
- Budget hotels - Rs 600-1200/night
- Mid-range - Rs 1500-3000/night
- Palace hotels - Rs 5000+/night

BUDGET TIPS:
- Street food very cheap (Rs 30-50)
- Auto rides Rs 10-30
- Many monuments have minimal entry fees
- Markets offer good bargaining opportunities
