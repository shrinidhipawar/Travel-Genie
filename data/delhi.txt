DELHI TRAVEL GUIDE

OVERVIEW:
Delhi is the capital of India with a mix of Old Delhi (historic) and New Delhi (modern). It offers rich history, magnificent monuments, and diverse food culture.

TOP ATTRACTIONS:

OLD DELHI:
1. Red Fort - Historic 17th-century fort with impressive walls and architecture
2. Jama Masjid - One of the largest mosques in India with stunning domes and minarets
3. Chandni Chowk - Bustling historic bazaar with shops, temples, and street food
4. Raj Ghat - Memorial to Mahatma Gandhi on the banks of Yamuna River

NEW DELHI:
1. India Gate - Iconic war memorial with surrounding gardens and parks
2. Rashtrapati Bhavan - Official residence of the President of India
3. Humayun's Tomb - UNESCO World Heritage Mughal monument
4. Qutub Minar - 12th-century minaret with Indo-Islamic architecture
5. Lotus Temple - Bahai temple with white marble petals, peaceful atmosphere
6. Laxmi Narayan Temple - Historic Hindu temple in New Delhi

RESTAURANTS & FOOD:
- Chole Bhature - Spiced chickpeas with fried bread
- Butter Chicken - Creamy tomato-based chicken curry
- Momos - Tibetan dumplings available in many parts
- Dosa - South Indian crepe with sambar and chutney
- Aloo Parathas - Stuffed flatbread with potato
- Biryani - Fragrant rice dish
- Street food: Chaat, gol gappas, samosas, jalebis
- Restaurants: Karim's (Old Delhi), Gulati (butter chicken), Indian Accent (fine dining)

SHOPPING:
- Connaught Place - Premium shopping and dining
- Delhi Haat - Government handicraft market
- Chandni Chowk - Budget shopping for electronics and bangles
- Khan Market - Designer shopping with restaurants
- DLF Mall - Modern shopping mall
- Sarojini Nagar - Budget shopping for clothes
- CP (Connaught Place) - High street fashion

TRANSPORT:
- Delhi Metro - Fastest and most efficient transport
- Autos/Cabs - Ola and Uber available
- Buses - DTC buses cover the city
- Taxis - Available at hotels
- E-rickshaws in some areas

MUSEUMS:
- National Museum - Artifacts from Indian history
- National Gallery of Modern Art - Contemporary art
- Indian War Memorial - Military history
- Rail Museum - Historic trains and locomotives

NEARBY DAY TRIPS:
- Agra (Taj Mahal) - 3-4 hours by train or road
- Jaipur (Pink City) - 5 hours by road
- Mathura - 60km, Krishna's birthplace

BUDGET TIPS:
- Street food is cheap (Rs 20-50)
- Budget hotels Rs 800-1500/night
- Metro is very affordable (Rs 10-60 per journey)
- Many museums have free or minimal entry
